Miss Brannigan's Creative Writing Class

Characters

Joe

Chriss

Elliott

Karen

Miss Brannigan

The scene is a a classroom at a community centre

Joe: Miss Brannigan is late this evening.

Chris: Yes, very unusual for her. She is normally here before us.

Karen: Yes, it's twenty past six now.

Elliot: I hope she is ok.

Joe: So how has everybody done this week?

Karen: I have hardly written anything.

Elliott: Nor have I.

Joe: I feel bad because she tries so hard to make us creative but I have just been blank this week.

Chris: Yes me too.

Joe: I just can't think of an original idea.

Karen: Yes, I am the same. That's the tough bit isn't it?

Elliot: Same with me. I sometimes thing every original story has already been written.

Chris: (Laughing) Yes. I know it isn't true but I feel like that as well.

Karen: All I keep getting into my head is some soppy love story. And all that has been done so many times before.

Joe: I'd like to write a thriller but I just can't get the right inspiration.

Karen: I am finding it difficult but I don't want to give up.

Elliot: I certainly don't want to give up. Ever since I was a kid I have wanted to be a writer.

Joe: Yeh, me too.

Chris: This is bad that none one of us has come up with an idea.

Karen: I hope Miss Brannigan won't be disappointed with us. She is so lovely.

Joe: Even if one of us had an idea we could have kind of shared it. Put our own slant on it.

Elliot: Yes that would have worked.

Chris: I guess we will all have to try extra hard next week.

Joe: Easier said than done.

Karen: I have even asked the people I work with if they have any ideas.

Elliott: I did that too but it didn't work. One or two people offered suggestions but they weren't really workable.

Joe: Sometimes I think I have a really good idea, then realised I have copied it from a book or movie.

Chris: Yes that is so easy to do.

Karen: We wouldn't be the first though. I have read so many books and seen so many movies where the story has been very close to another.

Joe: Yes that's very true.

Elliott: I actually find it quite easy to write the characters once I have the plot.

Karen:(laughing) I find that difficult too.

Joe: I think it is easier than finding the plot.

Chris: Definitely. The plot is definitely the hardest part.

Karen: The hardest part will be telling Miss Brannigan that not one of us has had an idea.

Elliott: Yes, I am not looking forward to that. I hate to disappoint her.

Joe: She did say right at the beginning that she could not give us ideas, we had to think those out for ourselves.

Karen: And she is right to do that.

Elliott: She can help us make it sound better but we have to supply the idea in the first place.

Joe: Exactly and we are failing miserably at that.

Karen: I am not going to give up though.

Joe: Not me. I am sure the creative juices will flow again.

Chris: Like a river I hope.

(they all laugh)

Joe: I guess we all have a lot to learn which is why we are taking this class.

Karen: And although we are all stumped at the moment, it's fun isn't it?

Chris: Yes definitely, I am really enjoying it.

Elliot: I think we all are.

Karen: Do you think she is coming this evening? She is awfully late.

Chris: Shame we don't have a contact number for her.

Joe: I do hope she is not unwell.

Karen: Oh I really hope not.

Elliott: I was just thinking. We shouldn't waste this time?

Chris: What do you mean?

Elliott: Well, we are all sitting here complaining that we don't have any ideas. We could be using this time constructively.

Joe: In what way?

Elliott: Lets put our heads together and maybe between us we could come up with a good plot?

Karen: Hey! That's a great idea. And you are right, we are sitting here doing nothing when we could be productive.

Joe: Ok, I can see how that might work.

Chris: It will certainly give us something to do while we wait for Miss Brannigan.

Elliot: Exactly. We have nothing to lose.

Joe: So how shall e start this?

Karen: I have an idea. How about we all think back to, well, maybe, a funny incident in our lives, and if there is a really good one we'll use as a basis for a story. We can take it in turns.

Elliot: Yes! I like it. Who goes first?

Joe: I think Karen should go first as it was her idea.

Karen: Ok I will. Let me Think

Chris: I hope I can remember something.

Joe: Yeh, may not be all that easy.

Karen: Ok, I've got something.

Elliott: Great, let's hear it then.

Karen: Ok. About 3 years ago I had parked my car in a supermarket carpark.

Elliot: Which one, it could help with the story.

Karen. Yes, of course. It was waitrose.

Joe: (laughing) Oh very posh, I have to go to Tesco's.

Karen: Me too normally. Anyway I have two big bags of shopping, and it is raining.

Chris: Well, this is England.

Karen: And I am trying to get my key from my coat pocket without dropping the shopping bags, and getting very wet.

Karen: Eventually I manage to dislodge the keys from my pocket but they fly out of my hand.

Joe: Oh no!So I have to put my shopping bags down on the wet ground to search for the keys.

Elliot: Were the bags paper or plastic?

Karen: Luckily they were plastic.

Chris: One good thing then.

Karen: Anyway, I can't see the keys anywhere, which is crazy because they couldn't have gone that far. I realized then what must have happened to them.

Joe: What?

Karen: When they flew out of my hands they must have gone under the car next to mine.

Elliott: Oh! Bummer, and the ground is very wet I am guessing?

Karen: You got it.

Chris: (Laughing) This is good.

Karen: It wasn't at the time. So, only one thing for it, I have to get on my knees, in the wet and grovel under the car to try and find my keys.

Joe: Oh my gosh! Poor you.

Karen: So I kneel on the wet concrete, feel under the car and my fingers just reach the keys.

Chris: So the day is saved.

Karen: Well not quite. I try to open my door then realise it isn't even my car, just one that looks the same as mine.

(all laughing).

Elliott: Good story Karen. What does everyone think? Could we work with that.

Joe: Well the thing is, That's Karen's story. Is it fair that we all use it?

Karen: (loudly) Oh! Miss Brannigan. Hello. We were worried about you.

Miss Brannigan: Oh no need to worry about me my dears. I am fine.

Joe: That's good. We were a bit concerned.

Miss Brannigan: Let me sit myself down and apologise to you all for being so late.

Karen: Everything is alright though isn't it Miss Brannigan.

Miss Brannigan: Yes, everything is fine thank you Karen. In fact couldn't be better.

Chris: That's good to hear.

Joe: You are usually here before us so that was whyworried a little.

Miss Brannigan: I do assure you everything is fine.

Elliott. That's a relief to all of us.

Miss Brannigan: Thank you Elliott. It's knd of ypou to say that. Now, People how have you been getting on this week?

(There is a short silence)

Elliot: Actually Miss Brannigan, we haven't been getting on very well at all.

Miss Brannigan: I see and why is that?

Karen: We are all in the same boat: None of us can think of a good subject matter for a story.

Chris: We just can't seem to get a good workable idea.

Miss Brannigan: In my opinion Chris, almost any idea is workable if you take it in the right direction.

Elliott: That is a thought worth hanging on to.

Chris: we feel bad because we don't want to let you down. You have helped us ll so much the we really do want to produce something good.

Miss Brannigan: And I am confident you will.

Joe: Well I admire your confidence in us.

Karen: Yes so do I.

Miss Brannigan: I have something to tell you all.

Karen: (surprised) What is it?

Elliott: You are not leaving us are you Miss Brannigan?

Miss Brannigan: ( A little laugh) Oh no, nothing like that at all.

Chris: Phew! That's a relief.

Miss Brannigan: How could I leave my darling little fledglings. My futer writers.

Joe: So what is it?

Miss brannigan:It is a little confession actually.

(Together) Chris, Karen, Elliot and Joe: A confession?

Miss Branigan: Yes indeed. I confess that I have been the tiniest bit deceitful.

Karen: In what way?

Elliot: Ha I can't imagine you being deceitful.

Miss Branigan: Well I am afraid I have been.

Karen: How?

Miss Brannigan: Well firstly I wasn't really late.

Joe: Were you here before us then – and left?

Miss Brannigan: No, I was actually here a little later than normal but only by a few minutes. I wanted you to all be here and chatting before I arrived.

Elliott: Which we were.

Karen: So were where you?

Miss Brannigan: ( another little laugh)I was right outside the door.

Chris: But why? What were you doing there?

Miss Brannigan: Listening to you all of course.

Joe: Oh dear so you heard out dilemma.

Miss Brannigan: I heard you all talking but I didn't spot any Dilemma.

Chris: You must have heard. Our joint dilemma is that we don't have a decent idea for a story between us.

Karen: We really have tried but all failed miserably.

Miss Brannigan: I don't think you failed at all.

Elliot: But we have, and also we all felt we have let you down a bit.

Miss Brannigan: Not at all. Tell me have you all hear the expression 'Can't See The Wood For The Trees'?

Joe: Yes of course.

Chris: I use it all the time.

Miss Brannigan: Good, because that is your only problem.

Karen: What do you mean?

Miss Brannigan: Over the last month or two you have all turned in some excellent work. You are all writers in the making.

Joe: If only we could get ideas for plots.

Miss Brannigan: And that is why I sat outside the door today. You see many good stories begin with simple ideas and conversations. I wanted to help you with that.

Chris: I wish you had.

Miss Branngan: Ah, but you see, I did.

Karen: How?

Miss Brannigan: I wanted you to see how an idea can develop from almost any situation.

Joe: Have I missed something here?

Miss Brannigan: You have indeed Joe.

Elliott: What did we miss then?

Miss Brannigan: What you actually missed – was that you had a plot.

Karen: Did we?

Miss Brannigan: Yes. A plot for a short story. And this is what I wanted to stress to you all. Plots don't have to be complicated, they can be quite simple situations.

Elliot: Oh! My gosh! I think I get it.

Miss Brannigan: Then please share it with the others Elliot and use this idea in the future.

Karen: What is it Elliott? Tell us.

Joe: Yes come on mate, spill the beans.

Elliot: We are the plot.

Chris: What?

Karen: Oh yes! I see it now too.

Miss Brannigan: What is it then Karen?

Karen: (Laughing) As Elliot said. We are the plot! Four people taking a creative writing class, talking before the tutor arrives about not being able to think of a plot. That's the story!

Joe: Oh yes of course!

Miss Brannigan: Well done, all of you. So here endeth today's lesson.

THE END
